2 Design steps

An Multipath amplifier consist of the amplification paths, the input network that divided the signal between the paths and an output network that combine the signals again.
To make the design easier, unconditional stable amplification paths are designed, which then normally ensure that the whole amplifier is also unconditionally stable. Thereafer, the input network is designed to match the 50 Ohm input 1) to the optimum input impedances (for minimum noise) of the amplifier paths and 2) to terminate it (small S 11 ). Lastly, the output network is designed to combine the signals of the paths in the correct ratio, to achieve a flat gain profile and to terminate the output.
Components and models used:
  • Double-sided 0.8mm FR4 PCB (Substrate definition), with the bottom layer a ground plane and the components and microstrip lines on the top layer.
  • 0603 NPO capacitors that are modeled as a capacitor with serie inductance and resistance (0603C0.ckt) . A lookup table is used for each discrete capacitance value to determine the series resistance.
  • 0603 wire wound inductors that are modeled as an inductor with series resistance and parallel resistance and capacitance (0603L0.ckt) . A lookup table is used for each discrete inductance value.
  • 0603 resistors is used as passive components.
  • ATF35143 HEMTs are used as active devices. Above 4 GHz, the manufacturers S-parameters are used and below 4 GHz the S-parameters are interpolated and extrapolated using a simple FET model.
